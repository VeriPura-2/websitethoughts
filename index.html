The Problem

One wrong field.
One rejected container.

The global food import system runs on paper — and it's failing the people who depend on it. For importers, forwarders, and carriers, the cost of compliance failure is measured in detained shipments, regulatory fines, and lost business. Today, importers may spend €50K or more on freelance compliance consultants just to navigate what VeriPura handles automatically.

$30K–$50K
Per FDA Detention Incident
An FDA automatic detention triggered by a documentation error can cost tens of thousands in warehouse fees, re-inspection costs, and lost product — before the goods ever reach a buyer.
14,000+
EU Food Import Refusals in 2024
EU border control refusals reached over 14,000 for animal-origin food products in 2024 alone, per the TRACES Annual Report. New regulations including EU 2017/625 and EUDR continue to raise the documentation bar that importers must clear.
"Hours to weeks"
FDA Examination Time Per Shipment
The FDA states that shipment examination can take "from a few hours to several weeks" if laboratory analysis is required. Every day a container sits at port compounds storage fees, spoilage risk, and buyer penalties.
14 Days
Average Contamination Identification Time
When contamination occurs, identifying the source through fragmented paper records takes an average of 14 days — by which point the product has long since reached consumers. VeriPura's immutable chain of custody compresses this dramatically.

Use Cases

Real shipments.
Real compliance problems solved.

See how VeriPura works across the food categories most exposed to documentation failure at EU and US borders.

All shipment scenarios are illustrative examples, not real shipments or clients.

Fresh Produce
Thai Mango to Rotterdam
Thailand → NLCPI Gap: 44pts

A Dutch importer sources 12 containers of fresh mango from a Thai cooperative. The shipment requires a phytosanitary certificate, pesticide residue analysis, a certificate of origin, and pre-export TRACES notification — all issued by different Thai authorities, in Thai and English.

Without VeriPura: Authority signature mismatch on phytosanitary cert caught at Rotterdam border control. 6-day hold. Substantial demurrage, re-inspection fees, and product loss — mango downgraded and partially destroyed.
With VeriPura: Signature mismatch flagged at Intake stage. Corrected cert obtained before departure. Container clears Rotterdam on arrival. Zero demurrage.
Seafood
Vietnamese Shrimp to Hamburg
Vietnam → DEEUDR Affected

A German seafood distributor imports 8 tonnes of frozen shrimp from a Vietnamese processor. Documentation required includes health certificates, catch certificates, cold chain logs, HACCP compliance records, and EU import notification via TRACES NT — across 5 issuing authorities.

Without VeriPura: Cold chain log format not accepted by TRACES NT system. Shipment flagged for enhanced border checks. 4-day delay, substantial costs incurred. Buyer relationship damaged.
With VeriPura: Cold chain log format issue identified at Parse stage. Supplier provided corrected format within 24 hours. Shipment pre-cleared via TRACES before vessel arrival.
Processed Food
Indian Spices to US FDA
India → USFSMA 204

A US specialty food importer sources cumin, turmeric, and chilli powder from three Indian suppliers. Each shipment requires FDA Prior Notice, FSMA traceability records, a Certificate of Analysis, and fumigation certificates — with strict field formatting required for FDA PREDICT system acceptance.

Without VeriPura: Prior Notice entry rejected by FDA PREDICT due to incorrect product description code. Shipment placed on automatic detention. Storage, re-examination, and bond costs in the range of $30K–$50K — consistent with FDA detention incident data.
With VeriPura: Product code mismatch identified at Comply stage before submission. Corrected Prior Notice accepted first time. No detention. FSMA traceability records blockchain-attested for audit.
Beef & Red Meat
Brazilian Beef to Great Britain
Brazil → GBEUDR AdjacentCPI Gap: 42pts

A British meat importer sources chilled and frozen beef cuts from a Brazilian processor in Mato Grosso do Sul. The shipment requires a UK health certificate issued by MAPA, an Export Health Certificate validated by APHA, deforestation due diligence records under the UK Forest Risk Commodities framework, cold chain documentation, and an EU–Mercosur-aligned certificate of origin — each with strict cross-referencing requirements between issuing authorities.

Without VeriPura: APHA rejects the Export Health Certificate at Tilbury — slaughter establishment number on the health cert does not match the approved UK establishment list. Shipment held for 9 days pending re-verification. Significant costs in cold storage, re-inspection, and partial product loss. Deforestation traceability records requested manually by UK Border Force, causing further delay.
With VeriPura: Establishment number mismatch and deforestation documentation gap both flagged at Comply stage before departure from Santos. Corrected certificates obtained within 48 hours. Shipment arrives Tilbury pre-cleared. Cold chain integrity maintained. IOTA-attested deforestation records available to UK Border Force on request — zero manual chase.
