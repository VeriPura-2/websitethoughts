About VeriPura

VeriPura is a pre-shipment food import compliance verification service. It checks every import document — phytosanitary certificates, health certificates, certificates of origin, and regulatory declarations — against EU, US, and UK border requirements before a container departs. Food importers, freight forwarders, carriers, and customs brokers use it to prevent FDA detentions, EU border rejections, and documentation-driven shipment delays.

FAQ

Common questions about food import compliance verification.

Questions from food importers, freight forwarders, carriers, and customs brokers about how VeriPura works and where it fits.

What is VeriPura?

VeriPura is a pre-shipment food import compliance verification service. It validates import documentation — phytosanitary certificates, health certificates, certificates of origin, certificates of analysis, and regulatory declarations — against EU, US, and UK border requirements before a container departs, using a 9-stage AI compliance workflow.

How does VeriPura prevent FDA automatic detentions?

FDA automatic detentions are typically triggered by documentation errors — an incorrect product code in Prior Notice, a missing field, or an authority mismatch. VeriPura checks every relevant field against FDA FSMA requirements before the shipment departs and flags issues for correction. A detention can cost $30,000–$50,000; the same error fixed before departure costs a corrected document.

Which import documents does VeriPura check?

VeriPura verifies phytosanitary certificates, health certificates, certificates of origin, certificates of analysis, common entry documents (CEDs), FDA prior notice submissions, fumigation certificates, and other documentation required by the destination market's regulatory framework.

What regulatory frameworks does VeriPura cover?

VeriPura covers EU Regulation 2017/625, FDA FSMA, EUDR (EU Deforestation Regulation), FSMA 204 traceability requirements, TRACES NT, and UK FRCR. Additional frameworks are available on request.

How does VeriPura help freight forwarders?

Freight forwarders use VeriPura to batch-process compliance checks across multiple active shipments simultaneously. VeriPura flags expiry dates, missing signatures, and authority mismatches, and generates client-facing compliance reports automatically. Every check produces a blockchain-attested record — protecting forwarders from client disputes when shipments are rejected.

How do freight forwarders offer VeriPura compliance verification to their clients?

Freight forwarders join the VeriPura channel partner programme and offer pre-shipment document validation as a value-added service alongside their freight offering. When a client's shipment is ready to move, the forwarder submits the document bundle through VeriPura. VeriPura checks every document against the destination market's requirements — FDA FSMA, EUDR, TRACES NT, EU 2017/625, FSMA 204 — and returns a compliance verdict with any flagged issues before departure. The forwarder receives a blockchain-attested compliance record for every check performed, which can be provided to the importer client as part of the shipment dossier.

How does VeriPura help carriers and customs brokers reduce customs holds on food shipments?

Customs holds and port delays on food shipments are most commonly triggered by documentation errors that could have been caught before departure. VeriPura gives carriers and customs brokers a pre-arrival compliance check: document bundles are validated against the requirements of the destination port and regulatory regime before the vessel sails. Errors that would trigger an FDA detention, a TRACES NT rejection, or an EU border hold are identified and flagged while they are still fixable. VeriPura also produces a blockchain-attested audit trail for every check — giving customs brokers a documented record of compliance diligence if a shipment is later disputed.

What does the VeriPura channel partner programme offer freight forwarders and carriers?

The VeriPura channel partner programme gives freight forwarders and carriers three things: a pre-shipment compliance verification service they can offer food importer clients as a value-added product; a revenue share on every shipment verified through their channel; and a blockchain-attested compliance record for every check that materially reduces their liability exposure when clients face border disputes. Partner slots in the founding pilot are limited. Founding partners set the terms of the programme they want built — the corridors, document types, and integration with their existing systems that matter most.

How does VeriPura help with EUDR compliance for food imports?

The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) requires importers to demonstrate that specific commodities — including beef, soy, cocoa, coffee, and palm oil — have not contributed to deforestation. VeriPura checks EUDR due diligence documentation as part of its pre-shipment workflow, flagging gaps before departure.

How does the VeriPura workflow produce a compliance verdict?

VeriPura processes every shipment through 9 stages: Intake (document upload), Mapping (HS code and corridor identification), Parse (AI field extraction), Trace (supply chain linkage), Comply (EU and FDA rule check), Shield (risk scoring), Auditor (audit trail generation), Verdict (pass, fail, or fix with remediation guidance), and Ledger (IOTA blockchain anchor).

Is VeriPura a replacement for a customs broker or compliance consultant?

No. VeriPura automates document validation and compliance checking against known regulatory requirements. It is used by importers, forwarders, carriers, and brokers — not as a replacement for professional customs or legal advice. It handles the systematic, repeatable part of compliance: checking that the right documents are present, correctly completed, and consistent with each other and with regulatory requirements.

What is TRACES NT and why do food importers use it?

TRACES NT (Trade Control and Expert System) is the European Commission's online platform for managing health and phytosanitary certificates for food imports entering the EU. Importers must submit Common Entry Documents (CEDs) via TRACES NT before shipments arrive at EU border inspection posts. Errors in document format, missing fields, or mismatches between the CED and the physical consignment are among the most common causes of EU food import refusals. VeriPura validates documentation against TRACES NT requirements before departure — so that CEDs are accepted on arrival rather than flagged for enhanced border checks.

What does FSMA 204 require from food importers, and how does VeriPura help?

FDA Food Safety Modernization Act Section 204 requires importers of designated high-risk foods to maintain and provide traceability records covering key data elements at each critical tracking event in the supply chain — from farm or processor through to the point of import. These records must be available to the FDA within 24 hours of a request. VeriPura validates that the required traceability documentation is present, correctly formatted, and consistent with the shipment before it departs — and anchors the records to the IOTA blockchain so they are immediately retrievable if FDA requests them.

Can VeriPura check the documents my supplier sends before I submit them to customs?

Yes. Supplier document validation is one of the primary use cases for VeriPura. Importers upload the document bundle received from their supplier — phytosanitary certificates, health certificates, certificates of origin, certificates of analysis — and VeriPura checks every field against the destination market's requirements before the importer submits to customs or the shipment departs. Errors that would trigger a border rejection or FDA detention are caught at the point when they are still fixable: before the container moves.

What are the most common food import documentation errors that cause border rejections?

The most common documentation errors in food import compliance include: authority signature mismatches on phytosanitary or health certificates; incorrect or missing product description codes in FDA Prior Notice submissions; Common Entry Document format errors that TRACES NT rejects; cold chain log formats not accepted by destination market authorities; establishment numbers that do not match approved lists; certificates that were valid at issue but expired by the time the shipment arrives; and missing EUDR due diligence records for affected commodities. VeriPura checks for all of these against the specific requirements of the destination market and trade corridor before the shipment departs.

Which food commodities are affected by the EU Deforestation Regulation and what documentation does it require?

The EUDR applies to cattle, cocoa, coffee, palm oil, soy, wood, and derived products — including beef, chocolate products, coffee blends, palm oil derivatives, and soy-based ingredients commonly traded in food import shipments. Importers must provide due diligence statements demonstrating that the commodities were produced on land not subject to deforestation after December 2020, with supporting geolocation data and supply chain documentation. VeriPura checks that the required EUDR due diligence documentation is present and complete before departure, flagging gaps that would cause rejection at EU borders.
